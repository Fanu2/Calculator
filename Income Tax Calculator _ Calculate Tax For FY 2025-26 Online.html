Overview

Calculating income tax can often feel complex, with multiple income sources, deductions, and exemptions to consider. On top of that, selecting between the old and new regimes adds to the confusion.

Our Income Tax Calculator makes this process seamless by showing your tax liability instantly. It also provides a side-by-side comparison of both regimes, helping you choose the most beneficial one.

What is the Income Tax Calculator?

Income Tax Calculator is an online tool that helps you to quickly calculate your income tax for selected financial year under both the old and new regimes. Whether you're salaried, self-employed, or a senior citizen, use it to compare regimes, estimate taxes, and optimise savings as per the latest slabs announced in Budget 2025.

How to Use the ET Money Tax Calculator?

Just follow these simple steps to use the income tax calculator online:

Step 1: Enter Basic Details

Select the financial year from the dropdown, choose your age group to apply the correct income tax slab, and pick your income source, such as salary or self-employment.

Step 2: Fill Income Details

Enter your income details, including salary, business income, or other earnings like interest on deposits, savings, and rental income.

Step 3: Enter Deductions

Enter eligible tax-saving deductions under various sections, such as 80C, 80D, 80G,80E, etc.

Step 4: Get Results

After entering all the information, click “Continue” to instantly calculate your taxable income and total tax liability.

You can also compare the old vs the new tax regime to check which helps you save more tax.

Rebate u/s 87A

In the old tax regime, if your taxable income is under ₹5 lakhs, you can claim a rebate of ₹12,500 under Section 87A, which basically cancels out your tax. But once your income goes above ₹5 lakhs, the normal slab rates kick in.

In the new regime, you don’t get exemptions like HRA or LTA, and most deductions are also gone. So, your full income is taxed as per the slab. The good part is, if your taxable income is up to ₹12 lakhs, you still get a rebate under Section 87A, which means you don’t pay any tax.

How to Calculate Income Tax For Salaried Individuals? (With Example)

Here are the steps for the income tax calculation for a salaried individual:

Step 1: Calculate your gross taxable income

To calculate your gross taxable income, you need to compute your net salary after subtracting your deductions, such as standard deduction, HRA, LTA, and other allowances, from your gross salary.

Salaried individuals receive a flat standard deduction from their salary income i.e., ₹50,000 under the old tax regime and ₹75,000 under the new tax regime.

Now, you need to add the net salary to other income from different sources, such as interest income, capital gains from investments, and rental income, to come to your gross taxable income.

Gross taxable income = Income from salary + Income from interest + Income from rentals + Income from other sources

Step 2: Calculate the total tax deductions

Calculate your total tax deductions by adding up investments under various sections of the Income Tax Act, such as Section 80C (ELSS, PPF, NPS), 80E, 80G, and 80TTA under the old tax regime, and Section 80CCD(2) under the new regime.

Total tax deductions = Standard Deduction + Investments under Section 80C + Health insurance premium under Section 80D + Savings account interest under Section 80TTA + Home loan interest under Section 24(b) + Others

Step 3: Calculate the net taxable income

In this third step, calculate the net taxable income by subtracting the total tax deductions from the gross taxable income.

Step 4: Calculate your total tax payable

Finally, calculate your tax liability based on the slab rates.

However, if your income is upto the specified limit then you get rebate on it, and there will be no tax.

Simply put, individuals must calculate the tax payable under both tax regimes and decide which regime to select.

Also, the ability to switch between tax regimes depends on the nature of the income. Salaried individuals and pensioners with no business income can switch between the two tax regimes every assessment year, depending on their financial status. If you are a non-salaried taxpayer and opt for the new tax regime, you can do so only once in your lifetime. Once you make that choice, you cannot switch back to the old regime again.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the financial year (FY) and assessment year (AY)?

FY is when you earn income whereas AY is when taxes are assessed and filed for the previous FY.

What is the difference between exemption and deduction?

Exemptions are a part of your income on which tax is not applicable. For example, a part of your LTA (Leave travel allowance) is considered an exemption. Deductions, on the other hand, are expenses that help you reduce your taxable income. For instance, you can claim your investments in ELSS, NPS, and PPF as deductions under Section 80C to lower your net taxable income.

Which deductions/exemptions are not available under the new tax regime?

Under the new tax regime, all deductions under Chapter VIA such as 80C, 80CCC, 80CCD, 80D, 80DD, 80DDB, 80E, 80G, 80GG, 80GGA, 80GGC, are not available to the taxpayer. Also, if you are salaried, you can not avail of exemptions on allowances such as HRA and LTA.

How do I choose between the new and old tax regimes?

Compare potential deductions in the old regime against lower tax rates in the new regime.

How much tax should I pay on my salary?

Tax payable on salary is based on your total taxable income after all the deductions and exemptions, the income tax slab you belong to, and the tax regime you have opted for.

Does everyone have to file their income tax returns?

If the taxable income of the individual exceeds the basic exemption limit of Rs.2.5 lakh in a financial year, then it is mandatory to file the income tax return.

What details do I need to provide while e-filing my ITR?

Information required at the time of e-filling are as follows:

  • Basic details such as PAN number, Adhar number, and Address
  • Details of income earned from various sources such as salary, house property or any other sources.
  • Proof of payment of any advance tax or TDS (Tax deducted at source) information
  • Bank details held in the financial year
  • Details of deductions to be claimed under Chapter VIA

Know more about ITR e-filling.

When can I file my income tax returns?

Typically the last date for filing an income tax return is 31st July after the end of the financial year. But it may be further extended as per the government’s discretion.

How does the income tax calculator work?

The income tax calculator works on the information you enter, including income, exemptions, deductions, age and tax regime. Based on these, it calculates your tax liability as per the current income tax rules.
